Conversations

01

Vincent Lostanlen

Interview

Researcher at CNRS / LS2N Laboratory, Nantes. We spoke about machine listening, bird song classification and the limits of what algorithms can hear.

The conversation started from a simple question: can a neural network trained on spectrograms ever capture what a bird is saying? His answer was both technical and philosophical.

Conversation · Nantes, janvier 2026

Duration: 42' — Recorded via Zoom. Lightly edited.

02

Chapelier Fou

Interview

Musician and sound artist. We discussed field recording, the ethics of capturing animal sounds, and his ongoing project composing with bird calls as raw material.

« When I record a bird, I'm not capturing a sound — I'm interrupting a conversation I don't understand. » — Chapelier Fou, February 2026

Visits

01

Boston Birding Festival

Field visit

Three days at the Boston Birding Festival. 140 species recorded, including several rarities. Conversations with amateur and professional ornithologists about the long-term data they are collecting.

The festival is also a ritual — the same people, same paths, same skies, year after year. A living archive of disappearance.

02

MIT Media Lab —
Future Sketches

Lab visit

Visited the Future Sketches group to discuss data visualisation approaches for long-term ecological datasets. How do you represent a 40-year decline in a way that is both scientifically rigorous and emotionally legible?

Sketches

01

Spectrogram drawings

Drawing

A series of drawings made from bird song spectrograms. Each drawing translates a sound visualisation into a hand-drawn mark — slowing down the reading, making the shape of a call tangible.

About

Hi! My name is Béatrice Lartigue.
Since 2008, I work as a visual artist and educator — as a co-founder of Lab212 Collective.
I'm currently working on a project exploring the collapse of bird populations called "Whistle Blowers".
In April 2026, I'll have the chance to take part in the Villa Albertine residency research programme in Boston (USA).
This research journal is a record of my various thoughts, research and encounters throughout this project.
